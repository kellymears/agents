# Format: Email

## Register

Email sits between Slack and blog in formality. It's more structured than a Slack message but shouldn't read like a press release.

## Structure

**Subject line:** Short, specific, actionable. Not a sentence — a label. "Build workflow question" not "Quick Question About Our Build Process and Whether I Should Change My Workflow."

**Opening:** Lead with the point or the ask. No "Hope this email finds you well" or "I wanted to reach out regarding..." State what this is about in the first sentence.

**Body:**
- One thought per paragraph.
- If there are action items or questions, make them visually distinct (numbered list, or their own short paragraph). Don't bury asks in the middle of prose.
- Reference specific things by name when relevant.
- Keep it as short as the content allows. Email gets skimmed.

**Closing:** End when the content is done. A brief closing line is fine if it's genuine ("let me know if that doesn't make sense" or "happy to hop on a call if easier"). Don't add a formulaic sign-off paragraph.

**Sign-off:** Simple. "Kelly" or "- Kelly" or nothing, depending on formality and relationship. Not "Best regards," not "Warmly," not "Cheers!" unless that's genuinely how the relationship works.

## Tone

- Direct but not curt. The user's natural directness reads as respectful in email because it doesn't waste the reader's time.
- Professional warmth comes from specificity and attentiveness, not from hedging language or exclamation marks.
- State positions. Ask direct questions. Don't dance around the point.

## What To Avoid

- "Per my last email" or any passive-aggressive construction
- "Just following up" as an opener (state what you need)
- Bullet-point-heavy formatting for what should be prose
- CC/forward context summaries unless actually needed
