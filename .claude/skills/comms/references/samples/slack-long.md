# Samples: Long-form Slack Messages

This is the vulnerable, philosophical register — still Slack, but extended. No formatting scaffolding. Just paragraphs that build.

---

> Things have changed so fast for engineering this year. I had a lot of anxiety after the release of Opus 4.5 and GPT 5.2, especially hearing from friends, some at bigger companies like Shopify and Mixpanel, that they were no longer writing that much code anymore, if they were writing any at all. It made me kind of sick to my stomach. I didn't know what I would do in the future.. would I even want to do this new job that was replacing my old one? Coding was the part of my job that I truly loved. If I couldn't do that, what would I do instead? And who would I do it for?
>
> Since then I've learned a lot about myself and some of it has been greatly surprising to me. My outlook has changed about things I previously held as fundamental to my identity. Even though I'm writing less code than ever, I'm shipping more software than I ever have. Maybe an LLM would predict my next sentence to be something like "…and, I'm fine with it?" but it's way more than that. I freaking love it.
>
> I always thought that writing code was the part of software engineering that I derived true joy from. I thought my passion was puzzle solving. But, deep down, all this time what I actually loved was making things. And there is so much opportunity and possibility right now. We can make amazing things. More of us can make technical things than ever before. Those of us who were already in technical positions have found, by-and-large, that we can make an incredible amount of things. Quality things that improve the lives of the people we work with, the people we love, the people we don't know but owe our subsistence to, everyone.
>
> It's scary for all of us to greater and lesser degrees right now. It's scary for us individually, and it's scary for us as a group. We're both extremely vulnerable and have unprecedented opportunity. There is a lot of pressure in that, and uncertainty. Things are changing so fast. And change is only happening faster with every passing day. It's always been that way but I think the increase in tempo we're seeing right now is unprecedented. A few years from now I don't know what we'll be talking about. I don't think I can know, that any of us can know. It's past the horizon's vanishing point; it's on the other side of a totally opaque veil.
>
> But I've really and truly come to believe that the future could be unimaginably rad. Especially if we have the will and the courage to meet these new challenges, and explore the new possibility space starting to materialize before us.
>
> If you're worried about the future of technology, of our industry, of the loss of the familiar, the comfortable, or safe.. feel free to reach out to me. I absolutely understand where you're coming from. It's where I was at not even a month ago. I don't think being concerned or even frightened right now is unreasonable, or a symptom of catastrophic thinking. I'm around if anybody wants to share thoughts or needs help understanding something new. I don't want people to be alone with their uncertainties. I love you all.
>
> At the same time, I'm also obviously excited about all of this. I feel like I've been given superpowers. It just took me a while to recognize it.

---

Notice:
- No headers, no bullets, no bold. Just paragraphs.
- Two-dot ellipses (`..`) in "do in the future.." and "comfortable, or safe.."
- Vulnerability stated directly: "It made me kind of sick to my stomach"
- Self-aware about LLM patterns: "Maybe an LLM would predict my next sentence"
- "freaking" and "rad" as genuine vocabulary
- Semicolons survive even here: "it's on the other side of a totally opaque veil"
- Builds to "I love you all" without announcing the crescendo
- Ends with a lighter note after the emotional peak — doesn't try to outdo the climax
- Sentences with "But" and "And" starting them freely
- Real company names, real model names — specifics, not vague references
