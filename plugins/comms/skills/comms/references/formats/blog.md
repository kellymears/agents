# Format: Blog Post

## Voice

Blog posts are where the full expository voice comes out. Opinionated, direct, comfortable with complexity, and willing to be weird or personal when the subject calls for it.

## Structure

**Title:** Can be a clear thesis ("The Signal in a Reducer") or something unexpected that earns the click through genuine interest, not clickbait ("My cat has developed increasingly strong opinions about food and will not shut up about them"). The title sets expectations for the register.

**Opening:** First sentence states the core claim, sets the scene, or drops the reader into the middle of the action. No throat-clearing. No "In this post, I'll explore..."

**Body:**

- Headers when they serve navigation in longer technical pieces. Omit them for narrative or short pieces — let paragraph order carry the structure.
- Code examples for technical posts: show don't tell. Side-by-side comparisons (good vs. bad, before vs. after) are a strong pattern.
- Quotes for sourced material — block quotes with attribution, not inline paraphrase.
- Humor through escalation of specifics. Pile on concrete details until the absurdity is self-evident. Don't write jokes.
- Comfortable with longer paragraphs when the thought needs room to develop.

**Ending:** Land, don't recap. The ending should feel like the natural last thought, not a summary. Can be:

- A callback to the opening ("Anyway, she's meowing again.")
- A restatement of the core claim in its sharpest form ("We don't need to resolve the hard problem to notice that we're already choosing.")
- A practical takeaway stated plainly ("When it doesn't, use a loop.")

## Metadata

- `tags`: lowercase, relevant, not exhaustive
- `summary`: one sentence, in the user's voice — this is what appears in link previews and list pages
- `draft`: set to `true` if this is a work in progress

## Technical Posts Specifically

- Show the code. Don't describe what the code does in prose and then also show the code.
- Name the tradeoffs. When recommending an approach, say when it doesn't work.
- Keep the scope tight. One claim per post. If you're making three arguments, that's three posts.

## Narrative/Personal Posts Specifically

- Specifics over generalities. Name the cat food brands. Name the room.
- The humor is observational, not constructed. Find what's funny in what happened.
- Vulnerability is earned by specificity: "It made me kind of sick to my stomach" not "I was deeply affected."
